Tourism in Australia

A story across regions, seasons, purposes, and economies - connecting home and abroad

National Overview

Tourism across Australia

Tourism plays a vital role in shaping local economies and communities across Australia. This map presents short-term visitor arrivals adjusted for population, allowing for fair comparison between states and territories. The results show which regions experience the greatest relative tourism impact.

Select different years to examine how patterns of visitor concentration have shifted over time.

Destinations

Most Popular Regions by State

Within each state, tourism is concentrated in a small number of regions. This chart highlights the top ten destinations that attract the highest number of visitors, illustrating the uneven distribution of travel activity across Australia.

Click on a state name to view its leading regional destinations.

Seasonality

Tourism Activity Over Time

Visitor numbers in Australia fluctuate throughout the year due to weather patterns, school holidays, and global travel trends. The heatmap shows when activity peaks and declines, revealing the seasonal rhythm of domestic tourism across different states and quarters.

Look for consistent seasonal peaks and off-peak periods that reflect travel habits across years.

Purpose of Travel

Why People Travel Within Australia

Australians travel for many reasons — leisure, business, visiting friends and relatives, or other personal needs. These lines track how each purpose has evolved over time, reflecting broader social and economic changes that influence travel behaviour.

Use the dropdown to compare different travel purposes and observe changing trends through time.

Economic Contribution

Tourism’s Changing Role in the Economy

Tourism contributes significantly to Australia’s national income, though its impact varies year by year. The waterfall chart traces these fluctuations, highlighting the years that recorded the largest gains or declines in contribution to GDP.

Examine periods of strong growth and decline to understand how tourism responds to global and domestic events. Bars in blue represent years of increase, while those in red indicate decreases in tourism’s GDP contribution.

Domestic vs International

How Visitors Support the Economy

Both domestic and international tourism drive Australia’s economic performance. This comparison shows how each group contributes to total tourism spending, and how these proportions shift over time.

Compare the size of each bar to see when international tourism overtakes domestic activity and how recovery patterns differ.

Source Markets

Where International Visitors Come From

Australia’s inbound tourism is shaped by a small group of major source countries. This chart ranks the top ten markets, showing how international travel patterns evolve and which nations contribute the most visitors each year.
